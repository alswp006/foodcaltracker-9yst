# .impeccable.md

## Design Context

### Users
20-40 year-old Korean women managing diet and weight loss, personal training clients tracking macros, and office workers eating convenience store meals and delivery food who are frustrated with generic nutrition databases. They've abandoned MyFitnessPal due to inaccurate Korean food data and tedious manual entry, and need a friction-free way to log meals—ideally one photo instead of 10 taps.

### Brand Personality
**Direct, Efficient, Trustworthy.** Speak in simple, action-oriented Korean that mirrors Toss's conversational tone—no marketing fluff. Position accuracy as the core promise; users expect Korean food data to *just work* because they've been let down by generic apps. Acknowledge the pain of diet tracking ("피곤하지 않게") while making the solution feel inevitable, not begrudging.

### Aesthetic Direction
TDS (Toss Design System) 준수. @toss/tds-mobile 컴포넌트만 사용. 커스텀 미적 방향 없음—TDS의 미니멀 신뢰성과 밝은 컬러 억양이 건강·투명성 신호.

### UX Principles
1. **Photo-first friction removal**: Camera shutter on home screen; recognize and log in <2 seconds with zero disambiguation. Default to confidence mode (show only the top match) unless precision required.
2. **Instant visual feedback**: Ring UI shows remaining daily calories in real-time; every scan/photo updates the ring live so progress feels tangible.
3. **Korean food accuracy as default**: Leverage food delivery APIs (배달의민족, 쿠팡이츠) and 식약처 data to pre-populate the database; crowdsource edge cases. Never show generic "Korean rice bowl" — recognize specific dishes (육개장, 된장찌개).
4. **Seamless integration into existing routines**: Barcode scanning at GS25/CU/7-Eleven feels native, not bolted-on; delivery menu linking saves users a lookup step. No extra steps to authenticate or explain context.
